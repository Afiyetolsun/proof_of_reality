# Proof of Reality — Product

> Web3 oracle for the physical world. Cryptographic proof that an object exists, here, now. Not generatable by AI, not pre-recordable, not forgeable on the ground.

## Register

**brand** — the landing surface IS the product on first contact.

## Users

**Primary (right now, hackathon week):**
- ETHPrague 2026 judges (SpaceComputer, ETHPrague Future Society / Best Hardware tracks). Technical, time-poor, allergic to vapor. Five minutes per project, two of them on the landing.
- Hackathon attendees scanning project links between talks.

**Soon after:**
- Marketplace operators (auto, luxury, P2P rentals) needing anti-fake listings.
- RWA / DeFi protocols tokenizing physical assets.
- DePIN orchestrators wiring oracles for physical-world contracts.
- Verifiers (insurance adjusters, KYC officers) checking a single proof.

## Product purpose

We turn a 30-second iPhone scan or a B2B edge-camera capture into an on-chain artifact that can be independently verified by five signatures: satellite cTRNG (cosmic nonce), KMS co-signature (SpaceComputer Space Fabric), device key (Apple Secure Enclave or USB Armory ECDSA), Swarm content addressing, and Apple App Attest where applicable.

The backend is a thin economic proxy. The trust roots are public. If the backend is fully compromised, attackers can burn quota and gas, but cannot forge a valid scan.

## Brand voice

Engineer-honest. Names cryptographic primitives by name. Short sentences. Numbers when they're real, silence when they're not. Confident without hype, literary without flourish.

Reference touchstones: Anthropic research pages, Stripe Press, Linear's earlier landing era, Igalia. The opposite of crypto-bro and the opposite of SaaS.

One literary moment per page is allowed. More is too much.

## Anti-references

These are reflex patterns to refuse. If a draft drifts here, rewrite.

- **Crypto neon-on-black.** First-order trap for "space + crypto." We are dark, but not Solana / Sui / cyberpunk. No electric cyan. No hot pink.
- **SaaS gradient mesh.** Purple-to-cyan blurs as a default texture. Voxelio's family. We learn craft from Voxelio; we do not look like it.
- **Glassmorphism as default.** Backdrop-blur cards, frosted everything. Used rarely, never as a default surface.
- **Hero-metric template.** "2.4M scans" / "50+ partners" / giant-number-small-label. We have zero scans. We will not pretend otherwise.
- **Identical card grids.** Six identical icon + heading + paragraph cards in a 3×2 grid. The default failure mode of every landing on the internet.
- **Em dashes** (and `--`). Use commas, colons, semicolons, periods, parentheses.
- **Gradient text.** `background-clip: text` over a gradient. Decorative, never meaningful.
- **AI-slop reassurance copy.** "Empower your workflow," "Unleash the future," "AI-powered." If the sentence could survive a find-replace of the noun, delete it.

## Strategic principles

1. **Show, don't claim.** A live cosmic nonce ticking at the top of the page is worth a thousand "verifiable" adjectives. A real Gaussian splat orbiting in the hero is worth a thousand product mockups.
2. **Engineer-honest beats marketing-warm.** Judges in this venue read as engineers. So do most of our paying customers in year one.
3. **Density over space-filler.** Fewer sections, more in each. Negative space is deliberate, not a substitute for content.
4. **Cryptographic strings are the visual signature.** Every page has at least one mono-typeset hash or address rendered as a first-class design element, not a code-block afterthought.
5. **The page must outlast the hackathon.** No "ETHPrague 2026" timestamps in load-bearing copy. The track eyebrow is fine; an expiring tagline is not.

## What's specifically out of scope for v1

Email waitlist forms. iOS App Store CTA (no app yet). B2B sales-style "book a demo" funnel. Translations. Light theme. A pricing section.
